The Solar System consists of the Sun and the objects that orbit it, including eight planets, their moons, dwarf planets, asteroids, and comets. The Sun is a nearly perfect sphere of hot plasma and contains more than 99.8% of the total mass of the Solar System. Its gravity dominates the system and keeps all the planets in their orbits.

Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun and has a very thin atmosphere, causing extreme temperature fluctuations between day and night. Venus, the second planet, has a dense atmosphere of carbon dioxide and clouds of sulfuric acid, creating a strong greenhouse effect that makes it the hottest planet in the Solar System.

Earth, the third planet, has a breathable atmosphere and liquid water on its surface, making it the only known planet to support life. Mars, the fourth planet, is a cold desert world with evidence of ancient riverbeds and polar ice caps. It is home to the tallest volcano in the Solar System, Olympus Mons.

Jupiter, the fifth planet, is the largest in the Solar System and a gas giant composed mainly of hydrogen and helium. It has a strong magnetic field and dozens of moons, including Ganymede, the largest moon in the Solar System. Saturn, the sixth planet, is known for its extensive ring system made of ice particles and rocky debris.

Uranus, the seventh planet, is an ice giant with a blue-green color due to methane in its atmosphere. It rotates on its side, making its seasonal changes extreme. Neptune, the eighth planet, is a deep blue ice giant with strong winds and storms, including the fastest winds recorded in the Solar System.

In addition to planets, the Solar System contains dwarf planets like Pluto, small icy bodies in the Kuiper Belt, and millions of asteroids in the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter. Comets, composed of ice and dust, travel in elongated orbits and display tails when they approach the Sun.

The study of the Solar System helps scientists understand the formation and evolution of planetary systems. Observations are made using telescopes, spacecraft, and robotic missions, which provide insights into the composition, structure, and dynamics of celestial bodies.
